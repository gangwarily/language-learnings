## Focus Areas for This Worksheet

**Prepositions that ALWAYS take accusative:**
- fÃ¼r (for)
- um (around/at - time)
- durch (through)
- gegen (against)
- ohne (without)

**Prepositions that ALWAYS take dative:**
- mit (with)
- nach (after/to)
- von (from/of)
- zu (to)
- bei (at/with)
- aus (from/out of)
- seit (since)

**Two-way prepositions** (accusative for movement, dative for location):
- in (in/into)
- an (at/on)
- auf (on)
- Ã¼ber (over)
- unter (under)
- vor (before/in front of)
- hinter (behind)
- neben (next to)
- zwischen (between)

**Possessive pronouns** (for "her/their" when subject is feminine):
- Nominative: ihre
- Accusative: ihre (fem/neuter), ihren (masc), ihre (plural)
- Dative: ihrer (fem), ihrem (masc/neuter), ihren (plural) + -n on noun!
- Genitive: ihrer (all forms)

**Subordinate conjunctions** (verb at end!):
- nachdem (after - with clause)
- obwohl (although)
- weil (because)
- dass (that)
- wenn (if/when)
- ob (whether)

**Important distinctions:**
- nachdem + clause: "Nachdem ich gegessen habe, ..."
- nach dem + noun: "Nach dem Essen ..."
- danach = afterwards (adverb)
