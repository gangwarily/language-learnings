## Focus Areas for This Worksheet

**Relative Pronouns - Nominative (subject of relative clause):**
- Masculine: **der** (Der Mann, **der** dort wohnt)
- Feminine: **die** (Die Frau, **die** dort wohnt)
- Neuter: **das** (Das Auto, **das** dort steht)
- Plural: **die** (Die Leute, **die** dort wohnen)

**Relative Pronouns - Accusative (direct object in relative clause):**
- Masculine: **den** (Der Mann, **den** ich gesehen habe)
- Feminine: **die** (Die Frau, **die** ich gesehen habe)
- Neuter: **das** (Das Auto, **das** ich gekauft habe)
- Plural: **die** (Die Leute, **die** ich gesehen habe)

**Word Order in Relative Clauses:**
- Pattern: [noun], [relative pronoun] + [subject (if different)] + [other elements] + **[VERB AT END]**
- Example: Das Auto, **das** ich gestern gekauft **habe**, ist schnell.

**How to Know Which Case:**
- **Nominative:** The relative pronoun is the SUBJECT of the relative clause
  - "Der Mann, **der** mir geholfen hat" (he helped â†’ subject)
- **Accusative:** The relative pronoun is the OBJECT of the relative clause
  - "Der Mann, **den** ich gesehen habe" (I saw him â†’ object)
