## Answer Key with Explanations

### 1. I'm giving the old man a new book.
**Answer:** Ich gebe dem alten Mann ein neues Buch.

**Explanation:**
- "dem alten Mann" - Dative (indirect object: to whom are you giving?). Masculine noun "Mann" requires "dem" + weak adjective ending "-en"
- "ein neues Buch" - Accusative (direct object: what are you giving?). Neuter noun requires "-es" ending with indefinite article

---

### 2. She lives in a small apartment in the city.
**Answer:** Sie wohnt in einer kleinen Wohnung in der Stadt.

**Explanation:**
- "in einer kleinen Wohnung" - Dative (location/where, no movement). Feminine noun "Wohnung" requires "einer" + weak ending "-en"
- "in der Stadt" - Dative (location). Definite article feminine dative = "der"

---

### 3. We're going to the new restaurant with our good friends.
**Answer:** Wir gehen in das neue Restaurant mit unseren guten Freunden. (OR: Wir gehen ins neue Restaurant...)

**Explanation:**
- "in das neue Restaurant" - Accusative (movement/destination: going TO). Neuter noun requires "das" + weak ending "-e"
- "mit unseren guten Freunden" - Dative (mit always takes dative). Plural dative requires "-en" ending on both possessive and adjective
- Note: "ins" = "in das" (common contraction)

---

### 4. The child is playing with the red ball in the garden.
**Answer:** Das Kind spielt mit dem roten Ball im Garten.

**Explanation:**
- "mit dem roten Ball" - Dative (mit + dative). Masculine noun requires "dem" + weak ending "-en"
- "im Garten" - Dative (location). "im" = "in dem" (contraction)

---

### 5. I bought this expensive watch for my younger brother.
**Answer:** Ich habe diese teure Uhr für meinen jüngeren Bruder gekauft.

**Explanation:**
- "diese teure Uhr" - Accusative (direct object). Feminine with demonstrative "diese" requires weak ending "-e"
- "für meinen jüngeren Bruder" - Accusative (für always takes accusative). Masculine requires "meinen" + weak ending "-en"
- Past tense uses Perfekt: "haben" + past participle at end

---

### 6. After the long meeting, I spoke with my German colleagues.
**Answer:** Nach der langen Besprechung habe ich mit meinen deutschen Kollegen gesprochen.

**Explanation:**
- "nach der langen Besprechung" - Dative (nach + dative). Feminine requires "der" + weak ending "-en"
- "mit meinen deutschen Kollegen" - Dative (mit + dative). Plural dative requires "-en" on adjective
- Word order: Time element first, then verb in second position, subject after verb

---

### 7. The woman in the blue dress is standing next to the tall building.
**Answer:** Die Frau in dem blauen Kleid steht neben dem hohen Gebäude.

**Explanation:**
- "in dem blauen Kleid" - Dative (location/wearing). Neuter requires "dem" + weak ending "-en"
- "neben dem hohen Gebäude" - Dative (neben is a two-way preposition, here showing location). Neuter requires "dem" + weak ending "-en"

---

### 8. I need to talk to the new manager about an important project.
**Answer:** Ich muss mit dem neuen Manager über ein wichtiges Projekt sprechen.

**Explanation:**
- "mit dem neuen Manager" - Dative (mit + dative). Masculine requires "dem" + weak ending "-en"
- "über ein wichtiges Projekt" - Accusative (über + accusative when meaning "about"). Neuter requires "-es" ending
- "sprechen" goes to end (modal verb construction)

---

### 9. He comes from a beautiful village near the Swiss border.
**Answer:** Er kommt aus einem schönen Dorf nahe der Schweizer Grenze.

**Explanation:**
- "aus einem schönen Dorf" - Dative (aus + dative). Neuter requires "einem" + weak ending "-en"
- "der Schweizer Grenze" - Genitive or dative (nahe can take either, dative more common in spoken German). "Schweizer" is an unchanging adjective form

---

### 10. During the cold winter, we often drink hot tea.
**Answer:** Während des kalten Winters trinken wir oft heißen Tee.

**Explanation:**
- "während des kalten Winters" - Genitive (während + genitive). Masculine genitive requires "des" + weak ending "-en" + noun ending "-s"
- "heißen Tee" - Accusative (direct object). Masculine with no article requires mixed ending "-en"

---

### 11. I'm writing an email to my best friend about my new job.
**Answer:** Ich schreibe eine E-Mail an meinen besten Freund über meinen neuen Job.

**Explanation:**
- "eine E-Mail" - Accusative (direct object). Feminine
- "an meinen besten Freund" - Accusative (an + accusative when meaning "to" a person). Masculine requires "meinen" + superlative ending "-en"
- "über meinen neuen Job" - Accusative (über + accusative). Masculine requires "meinen" + weak ending "-en"

---

### 12. The students are learning German because they want to work in Austria.
**Answer:** Die Studenten lernen Deutsch, weil sie in Österreich arbeiten wollen.

**Explanation:**
- "weil" - subordinating conjunction, sends verb to end of clause
- "in Österreich" - Dative (location, no movement). Country names usually don't take articles
- Word order in subordinate clause: subject + objects + verb at end

---

### 13. She's waiting for her older sister in front of the modern museum.
**Answer:** Sie wartet auf ihre ältere Schwester vor dem modernen Museum.

**Explanation:**
- "auf ihre ältere Schwester" - Accusative ("warten auf" + accusative). Feminine requires "ihre" + strong ending "-e"
- "vor dem modernen Museum" - Dative (vor is two-way preposition, here showing location). Neuter requires "dem" + weak ending "-en"

---

### 14. I can't work without my strong coffee in the morning.
**Answer:** Ich kann ohne meinen starken Kaffee am Morgen nicht arbeiten.

**Explanation:**
- "ohne meinen starken Kaffee" - Accusative (ohne + accusative). Masculine requires "meinen" + weak ending "-en"
- "am Morgen" - Dative. "am" = "an dem"
- "nicht" comes before the infinitive "arbeiten" at the end

---

### 15. The books on the wooden table belong to the young professor.
**Answer:** Die Bücher auf dem hölzernen Tisch gehören dem jungen Professor.

**Explanation:**
- "auf dem hölzernen Tisch" - Dative (location, no movement). Masculine requires "dem" + weak ending "-en"
- "dem jungen Professor" - Dative (gehören + dative). Masculine requires "dem" + weak ending "-en"

---

### 16. We're traveling through the scenic mountains with a good guide.
**Answer:** Wir reisen durch die malerischen Berge mit einem guten Führer.

**Explanation:**
- "durch die malerischen Berge" - Accusative (durch always takes accusative). Plural accusative with definite article requires weak ending "-en"
- "mit einem guten Führer" - Dative (mit + dative). Masculine requires "einem" + weak ending "-en"

---

### 17. Despite the bad weather, the children played in the big park.
**Answer:** Trotz des schlechten Wetters spielten die Kinder im großen Park.

**Explanation:**
- "trotz des schlechten Wetters" - Genitive (trotz + genitive). Neuter genitive requires "des" + weak ending "-en" + noun ending "-s"
- "im großen Park" - Dative (location). "im" = "in dem", weak ending "-en"
- Simple past "spielten" is acceptable here

---

### 18. I met the famous artist at an interesting exhibition last week.
**Answer:** Ich habe den berühmten Künstler letzte Woche auf einer interessanten Ausstellung getroffen.

**Explanation:**
- "den berühmten Künstler" - Accusative (direct object). Masculine requires "den" + weak ending "-en"
- "letzte Woche" - Accusative (time expression, no preposition)
- "auf einer interessanten Ausstellung" - Dative (location, at an event). Feminine requires "einer" + weak ending "-en"

---

### 19. The cat is sleeping under the warm blanket on the comfortable sofa.
**Answer:** Die Katze schläft unter der warmen Decke auf dem bequemen Sofa.

**Explanation:**
- "unter der warmen Decke" - Dative (location, no movement). Feminine requires "der" + weak ending "-en"
- "auf dem bequemen Sofa" - Dative (location). Neuter requires "dem" + weak ending "-en"

---

### 20. He's giving his little daughter a beautiful flower from the colorful garden.
**Answer:** Er gibt seiner kleinen Tochter eine schöne Blume aus dem bunten Garten.

**Explanation:**
- "seiner kleinen Tochter" - Dative (indirect object). Feminine requires "seiner" + weak ending "-en"
- "eine schöne Blume" - Accusative (direct object). Feminine with indefinite article requires mixed ending "-e"
- "aus dem bunten Garten" - Dative (aus + dative). Masculine requires "dem" + weak ending "-en"

---

### 21. I need to send this important letter to my German colleague before Friday.
**Answer:** Ich muss diesen wichtigen Brief vor Freitag an meinen deutschen Kollegen schicken.

**Explanation:**
- "diesen wichtigen Brief" - Accusative (direct object). Masculine with demonstrative requires weak ending "-en"
- "vor Freitag" - Dative (vor + dative when temporal). Days don't take articles
- "an meinen deutschen Kollegen" - Accusative (an + accusative with "schicken"). Masculine requires "meinen" + weak ending "-en"

---

### 22. The manager spoke about the new project with great enthusiasm.
**Answer:** Der Manager sprach mit großer Begeisterung über das neue Projekt.

**Explanation:**
- "mit großer Begeisterung" - Dative (mit + dative). Feminine requires "großer" (strong ending because no article)
- "über das neue Projekt" - Accusative (über + accusative). Neuter requires "das" + weak ending "-e"
- Simple past "sprach" (irregular verb)

---

### 23. She's reading an exciting book that her teacher recommended.
**Answer:** Sie liest ein spannendes Buch, das ihr Lehrer empfohlen hat.

**Explanation:**
- "ein spannendes Buch" - Accusative (direct object). Neuter requires "-es" ending
- "das" - relative pronoun (nominative, refers back to "Buch"). Neuter subject of the relative clause
- "empfohlen hat" - Perfect tense in subordinate clause, "hat" goes to end

---

### 24. We bought fresh bread at the old bakery around the corner.
**Answer:** Wir haben frisches Brot in der alten Bäckerei um die Ecke gekauft.

**Explanation:**
- "frisches Brot" - Accusative (direct object). Neuter with no article requires strong ending "-es"
- "in der alten Bäckerei" - Dative (location). Feminine requires "der" + weak ending "-en"
- "um die Ecke" - Accusative (um + accusative). Fixed expression

---

### 25. The doctor explained to the worried patient that everything will be fine.
**Answer:** Der Arzt hat dem besorgten Patienten erklärt, dass alles gut wird.

**Explanation:**
- "dem besorgten Patienten" - Dative (indirect object with "erklären"). Masculine weak noun requires "dem" + weak ending "-en" + weak noun ending "-en"
- "dass" - subordinating conjunction, verb goes to end
- "gut wird" - future meaning with present tense "werden"

## Common Patterns to Remember

**Two-Way Prepositions (an, auf, in, über, unter, vor, hinter, neben, zwischen):**
- Accusative = movement/destination (Wohin?)
- Dative = location (Wo?)

**Always Accusative:** bis, durch, für, gegen, ohne, um, wider

**Always Dative:** aus, außer, bei, gegenüber, mit, nach, seit, von, zu

**Always Genitive:** (an)statt, trotz, während, wegen

**Adjective Ending Patterns:**
- Definite article (der/die/das): weak endings (-e or -en)
- Indefinite article (ein/eine): mixed endings
- No article: strong endings (take over article's job)
